Study methodology

The system comprised several groups of arrays (GAKs), each containing six strings of 21 identical modules. In three separate GAKs — GAK 4, GAK 8, and GAK 14 — one string was coated with SolarEX Titan while the remaining five strings in each GAK remained uncoated. Performance data was recorded through Smartblue Smart Control at 15-minute intervals.

The coating was applied on 13 October 2022 according to the recommended application procedure. Data collection ran from 8 February 2023 to 3 February 2024. The installation was regularly serviced, including biannual cleaning, and the study implementation was supervised and documented by Thilo Scharf, a publicly appointed and sworn expert in photovoltaic system technology.

Performance insights suitable for the website

  • The positive effect was reported as immediately visible after application.
  • Coated strings continued to outperform uncoated strings even after routine cleanings.
  • The report describes stronger performance around midday peak sunlight hours.
  • The report also describes a “jump-on” yield effect under lower irradiation conditions.

Financial implication block

The report states that each coated string generated an additional €128.94 per year in revenue. When extrapolated to the full 4,011-module installation, the study states a potential additional revenue of €24,627 per year. On the public website this should be framed explicitly as study-based financial implication, not as a universal revenue guarantee.

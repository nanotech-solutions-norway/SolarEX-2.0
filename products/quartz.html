SolarEX Quartz

SolarEX Quartz is the SiO₂ product pathway in the SolarEX portfolio. It is positioned around passive self-cleaning through hydrophobic and oleophobic surface behavior, reducing contaminant adhesion and simplifying maintenance. The uploaded Quartz evidence now adds a monitored rooftop field case from Hochdorf with a treated-versus-untreated comparison.

Quartz positioning summary

  • Transparent coating layer positioned at 100–150 nm
  • Application range framed at 5–15 mL/m²
  • Easy-clean, anti-stick, and durability narrative
  • Water-based formulation note relevant for warm climates

How Quartz works

The Quartz product story is built on a thin SiO₂ nano-film that reduces surface energy and supports water-beading and contaminant release. In commercial terms, this means easier cleaning, lower soiling persistence, and more stable solar panel performance over time.

Formulation pathway

The uploaded Quartz material indicates both alcohol-based and water-based formulation logic. Water-based variants are strategically relevant in warm climates where slower evaporation can improve coating laydown and uniformity.

What the Hochdorf case adds

The uploaded InterCos material is commercially valuable because it moves Quartz beyond abstract product positioning. The case documents a monitored rooftop system with 337 Huasun Himalaya M6 Frameless 390 W modules, total installed capacity of 131.43 kW, and a treated-versus-untreated comparison tracked from Jul-23 through Sep-25.

Within that setup, WR1 represented 127 treated south-facing panels, WR2 represented 146 treated north-facing panels, and WR3 represented 64 untreated control panels. The executive summary normalizes performance on a per-panel basis first and then aggregates the array-level energy gain, which is the right structure for a mixed-array comparison.

How to use this in sales conversations

  • Present Hochdorf as monitored field evidence, not as a universal guarantee.
  • Keep south and north treated sections distinct, because the uplift reported for WR1 and WR2 is materially different.
  • Use the portfolio figure when discussing full-site economics and the WR1 / WR2 figures when discussing array-specific behaviour.
  • Use the detailed case-study page when a prospect requests methodology, tables, or calculation logic.

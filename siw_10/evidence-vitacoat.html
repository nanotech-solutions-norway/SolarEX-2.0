PCS antimicrobial route

Direct evidence summary for VitaCoat-based positioning

This page consolidates the direct evidence narrative already embedded in SIW for the PCS antimicrobial route: technical composition, EN-series efficacy, supplementary respiratory-virus evidence, and non-floor wear persistence.

Technical composition

Product-route interpretation

Citrox + SiO₂ matrix

SIW positions VitaCoat as a hybrid surface route using citrus bioflavonoids embedded in a SiO₂ matrix on non-porous surfaces such as glass, metal, and plastic. Within the workspace this supports high-touch indoor hygiene applications rather than porous-substrate or heavy-floor-abrasion positioning.

Supplementary respiratory-virus evidence

Interpretation boundaries

Fast coronavirus and respiratory-virus support

SIW uses supplementary virus evidence to strengthen the route for enveloped respiratory viruses, while also keeping the non-enveloped-virus limitation explicit. This means the route should not be overstated as broad, universal virucidal proof across all virus classes.

Wear durability

Persistence on high-touch non-floor surfaces

Contact-angle retention framing

SIW uses the reported touch-durability and contact-angle retention results as persistence evidence for high-touch, non-floor surfaces. The route does not transfer that evidence into floor-grade wear proof, which remains a separate category with materially different abrasion severity.

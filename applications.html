Application fit

Select the coating mechanism based on the real contamination profile

The uploaded project material supports a clear positioning framework: SolarEX Quartz for passive hydrophobic easy-clean protection, and SolarEX Titan for active photocatalytic cleaning when organic fouling is a material operational problem.

Technology matching

Decision structure for project selection

SolarEX Quartz

Best fit for passive easy-clean and durable transparency

  • Dust, spotting, and general environmental fouling are the main loss drivers
  • Asset owners want lower cleaning frequency and less aggressive maintenance
  • High transparency and chemically stable surface protection are priority requirements
  • Hot-climate water-based formulation suitability is commercially relevant

SolarEX Titan

Best fit for active self-cleaning under sunlight

  • Organic residues, pollen, microbes, bird fouling, or biofilm are recurring issues
  • Daylight-driven contaminant decomposition provides operational value
  • Uniform water sheeting and streak reduction matter for rinsing quality
  • UV-protection and photocatalytic differentiation strengthen the project case

Representative environments

Solar asset profiles where SolarEX can create value

Utility-scale solar fields

Large panel counts, water consumption, and cleaning logistics make self-cleaning economics highly relevant.

Commercial rooftop systems

Access limitations and service constraints increase the value of reduced manual cleaning cycles.

High-dust and mixed-fouling climates

Sites with both particulate and organic contamination benefit from an explicit mechanism-based coating selection process.

Project workflow

Recommended commercial progression

1. Contamination assessment

2. Technology mapping

Align the project with SolarEX Quartz or SolarEX Titan based on mechanism fit.

3. Pilot and scale-up

Run a pilot area, validate yield and maintenance outcomes, then expand to broader deployment.

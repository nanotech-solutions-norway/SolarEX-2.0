Application fit

Select the coating mechanism based on the contamination profile, not on generic assumptions.

The SolarEX source material supports a mechanism-led decision structure. SolarEX Quartz is the passive, hydrophobic easy-clean pathway. SolarEX Titan is the active, photocatalytic daylight-cleaning pathway. The right product depends on what is actually happening on the site.

Site profileRecommended pathReasoning
Dust, spotting, general outdoor soilingSolarEX QuartzPassive hydrophobic and oleophobic easy-clean behavior is usually the strongest fit
Organic fouling, pollen, microbes, bird contaminationSolarEX TitanPhotocatalytic decomposition under sunlight creates more differentiated value
High water-use and cleaning-cost pressureSolarEX Quartz or TitanBoth pathways can reduce cleaning intensity, but the mechanism should follow the fouling type
Mixed-fouling solar fieldsPilot comparisonRun a structured field pilot to validate the higher-value mechanism on site

Quartz fit logic

Use Quartz when passive easy-clean protection is the priority

  • Utility-scale fields with high dust loading and routine surface contamination
  • Commercial rooftops where access difficulty makes reduced cleaning frequency valuable
  • Sites that prioritize transparent protection, durability, and lower water demand
  • Projects where passive repellency solves most of the performance-loss problem

Titan fit logic

Use Titan when sunlight-driven organic breakdown creates the advantage

  • Sites with recurring bird fouling, pollen, microbial contamination, or organic films
  • Assets where streak-free rinsing and superhydrophilic water behavior matter
  • Locations with strong daylight exposure that support photocatalytic activation
  • Commercial cases where active self-cleaning is easier to justify than passive repellency alone

Recommended commercial process

Start with the contamination profile

Map the dominant fouling mechanisms: particulate, mineral, organic, or mixed
Review current cleaning frequency, water use, access burden, and cost profile
Assess whether passive hydrophobic behavior or active photocatalytic action is the better first hypothesis
Plan a pilot area and validate against untreated reference panels

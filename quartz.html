SolarEX Quartz | SiO₂ nanocoating

Passive self-cleaning through hydrophobic and oleophobic surface behavior

SolarEX Quartz is an advanced SiO₂-based coating for photovoltaic panels. It forms a transparent nano-film that reduces contaminant adhesion, supports easier cleaning, and helps preserve panel efficiency through improved surface condition.

Mechanism

How SolarEX Quartz works

The coating uses silica nanoparticles in a glass-like matrix to create a stable, transparent surface that reduces surface energy and contaminant bonding.

01

Silica nanostructure

SiO₂ nanoparticles create a very high-surface-area nano-layer with strong bonding to glass substrates.

02

Hydrophobic effect

Water and oily residues are repelled, limiting retention and supporting beading-and-roll-off behavior.

03

Lotus-effect cleaning

Reduced adhesion means dirt is more easily removed by rain, dew, or light cleaning cycles.

Operational benefits

Why Quartz matters in solar operations

  • Strong anti-stick and easy-clean performance
  • High transparency with negligible impact on spectral transmission
  • Improved resistance to abrasion, weathering, and chemical exposure
  • Reduced maintenance cycles and gentler cleaning requirements
  • Protection against environmental degradation of panel surfaces
  • Available in water-based and alcohol-based formulation pathways

Use-case logic

Best fit profile

SolarEX Quartz is a strong fit where passive easy-clean behavior, high durability, and stable transparency are the principal requirements. It is particularly relevant for solar sites exposed to water spotting, dust accumulation, and routine environmental fouling.

Water-based versions are noted in the source material as advantageous in hotter climates due to slower evaporation and more uniform deposition.

Application

Representative application guidance

Surface cleaning

Remove residues completely before application to secure proper bonding and film formation.

Application method

Use HVLP fine-mist spray or cloth-wipe application depending on scale and site conditions.

Coverage control

Target thin and uniform distribution in the approximate 5–15 mL/m² range.

Curing

Allow rapid initial drying and a full curing window of around 24 hours before service exposure.

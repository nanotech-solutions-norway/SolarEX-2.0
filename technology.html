Technology overview

Two nanocoating pathways. Two different cleaning logics.

The SolarEX website architecture is built on a crucial distinction: Quartz uses a passive SiO₂ anti-stick / easy-clean mechanism, while Titan uses an active TiO₂ photocatalytic mechanism with superhydrophilic rinsing behavior.

DimensionQuartz / SiO₂Titan / TiO₂
Primary mechanismHydrophobic and oleophobic easy-clean surface behaviorPhotocatalytic breakdown of organic contamination
Cleaning modePassive release of dust, water, and contaminantsActive organic decomposition plus water sheeting
Water behaviorBeading / release behaviorSuperhydrophilic sheet flow after activation
Commercial fitDust, easy-clean, reduced maintenance narrativeOrganic contamination and proof-backed differentiation narrative
Common positioning riskOverstating performance as universalOversimplifying photocatalytic mechanism

Quartz technology logic

Quartz is built on SiO₂ nanostructure logic that supports reduced contaminant adhesion, easier natural cleaning, and maintenance reduction. The uploaded material also supports durability, transparency, and climate-specific formulation discussion.

Titan technology logic

Titan is built on TiO₂ nanostructure logic where light exposure drives reactive cleaning behavior. The uploaded Titan material further supports UV-related functionality, superhydrophilic rinsing, and a stronger long-study proof architecture.

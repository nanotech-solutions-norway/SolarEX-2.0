Proof stack and evidence typing

Measured study data, commercial framing, and reference-case output in one review layer.

The SolarEX proof stack is strongest when it separates three evidence classes: monitored study data, commercial ROI framing, and spreadsheet-backed or inverter-backed reference comparisons. This page is structured to make those distinctions explicit.

1. Monitored study evidence

The strongest measured dataset in the current source set is the TiO₂ rooftop study. It used three coated strings across GAK 4, 8, and 14, compared them against adjacent uncoated strings, and monitored current values at 15-minute intervals over 360 days.

  • Average gain: +5.15%
  • String results: +5.62%, +5.22%, +4.62%
  • Study system size: 1163.19 kWp rooftop system
  • Total modules in system: 4,011

2. Commercial deck framing

The commercial deck is not the same as a controlled field study. It combines electricity-value assumptions, sunshine-hour assumptions, coating price, and expected output uplift to frame payback periods and long-term value.

  • Quartz Europe framing: 2,335 sunshine hours/year, €0.289/kWh, 10% uplift, €2.44/m² coating cost
  • Titan high-insolation framing: 3,000 sunshine hours/year, €0.759/kWh
  • Use these figures for decision support, not as direct substitutes for monitored proof

3. Reference-case output data

The Hochdorf PDFs are useful because they show inverter-level totals and per-panel production rather than broad narrative claims. That makes them stronger than an unsupported testimonial, even though they are not a full formal study protocol.

  • WR1 treated south: 127 panels
  • WR2 treated north: 146 panels
  • WR3 untreated: 64 panels
  • Average yield without treatment in the summary: 425 kWh/panel
  • Average yield nano-treated in the summary: 504 kWh/panel

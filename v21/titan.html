SolarEX Titan | TiO₂ nanocoating

Photocatalytic self-cleaning for organic fouling on photovoltaic assets.

SolarEX Titan is the active self-cleaning route in the platform. Its value case is stronger when the site suffers from pollen, algae, bird fouling, or other organic contamination patterns that benefit from UV-driven degradation and superhydrophilic rinse behavior.

Study-backed evidence

  • Three coated strings across GAK 4, 8, and 14
  • 15-minute monitoring intervals
  • Measured string outcomes: +5.62%, +5.22%, +4.62%
  • Average monitored gain: +5.15%
  • Commercial extrapolation in the study: ~€24,627/year for the full rooftop system

Mechanism profile

  • Photocatalysis under UV exposure
  • Degradation of organic contamination on the surface
  • Superhydrophilic water-sheeting after activation
  • Useful where organic-fouling patterns dominate the loss profile

Where Titan is strongest

  • Pollen-rich seasonal environments
  • Bird-fouling exposure on rooftops or infrastructure
  • Sites with algae or organic surface contamination
  • Review processes that value monitored study evidence

Handling and deployment notes

  • Waterborne translucent system
  • Ethanol 1–<5% in SDS
  • Eye irritation hazard classification in SDS
  • Not classified as dangerous goods for transport
  • Use controlled application and documentation when building the monitored value case

What a good Titan pilot should prove

  • Persistent treated-vs-control separation over time
  • Better performance under organic-fouling conditions
  • Reduced visible residue or rinse-behavior improvement
  • A value case that can be linked to monitored uplift rather than only modeled uplift

From site intake to treated-versus-control validation.

Field application sequence

This sequence is derived from the Quartz application instructions and should be treated as the core process logic for panel preparation, solvent cleaning, coating laydown, and cure control.

1. Intake and work planning

  • Confirm panel type, age, tilt, and access method
  • Document the fouling profile with photos
  • Check weather window and surface-temperature exposure

2. Gross cleaning

  • Wash with approved non-residue cleaning route
  • Remove dirt load before solvent preparation stage
  • Rinse thoroughly with clean water

3. Deep preparation

  • Dry the surface fully, including panel edges
  • Use IPA or equivalent alcohol cleaner to remove fine contamination
  • Do not proceed on damp or humid surfaces

4. Coating laydown

  • Apply by wipe or spray route
  • Keep dosage within the operational range
  • Avoid direct sunlight and panel surfaces above 30°C

5. Flash-off and cure

  • Allow 5–30 minutes ready-cure depending on temperature
  • Buff haze away using clean microfiber
  • Allow full cure for 24 hours

Pilot design logic

  • Define treated and untreated control zones before application.
  • Capture baseline production, wash interval, and fouling condition before treatment.
  • Track yield, wash events, and any material operational differences over the pilot period.
  • Translate results into both output value and O&M effect rather than reporting only percentage uplift.

Success criteria

  • Measured production separation that persists over time
  • Reduced wash burden or longer acceptable wash interval
  • Visual fouling control that matches the dominant site mechanism
  • Commercial interpretation that survives internal finance review

Quartz-specific operational notes

  • Ideal application range: 15–20°C
  • Do not apply above 30°C surface temperature
  • Rain-resistance framing after 1 hour
  • Full cure after 24 hours

Titan-specific pilot note

  • Use Titan where organic fouling, pollen, algae, or bird contamination is structurally relevant.
  • The value case should be linked to measured uplift logic from the monitored study and then adjusted to local conditions.

Application engineering sequence for Quartz

The application-instructions document is unusually useful because it moves beyond broad product description and gives a field-sequence logic for panel preparation, solvent cleaning, wipe loading, and environmental constraints.

1. Gross clean

Wash with diluted BioSativa® at 1:15 where available, or use a surfactant-free alternative that leaves no residue.

2. Rinse and dry

Rinse thoroughly with clean water. Dry carefully, with attention to panel edges and drip-prone perimeter zones.

3. Alcohol deep clean

Remove residual contamination in micro-scratches using IPA or equivalent alcohol cleaner without polishing additives.

4. Apply coating

Use wipe or spray route. A 6 mL pre-impregnated wipe is framed for about 1.5 m²; a 150 × 150 mm lint-free wipe can be loaded with ~10 mL.

5. Flash off and buff

Allow 5–30 minutes depending on temperature, then buff haze away with a clean microfiber. Full cure is 24 hours.

Handling, storage, and safety

  • Quartz is a flammable ethanol-rich system. Keep away from heat, sparks, and open flames.
  • Titan is not transport-classified as dangerous goods in the SDS, but still requires splash/eye protection and controlled handling.
  • Both SDS files recommend nitrile or butyl gloves with at least 0.7 mm thickness and breakthrough time greater than 480 minutes for repeated handling.
  • Both systems require good ventilation and should not be allowed to enter surface water or drains.

Mechanism and deployment fit

  • Quartz is the stronger route where low adhesion, hydrophobic shedding, and wash-cycle reduction dominate the business case.
  • Titan is the stronger route where UV-driven decomposition of organics, superhydrophilic sheet flow, and monitored self-cleaning behavior are decision-relevant.
  • The TiO₂ study confirms measured yield uplift; the TiO₂ technical deck extends the mechanism case into UV protection, contaminant degradation, and biofilm-control framing.

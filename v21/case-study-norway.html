What this case can support

  • Treated-vs-control pilot logic for buyer evaluation
  • Evidence-backed discussion of wash-burden and output retention
  • More disciplined proof visuals for commercial decks and website pages
  • Internal discussion with technical and procurement stakeholders

What this case should not claim

  • It is not a formal peer-reviewed study protocol.
  • It should not be presented as universally representative of every site type.
  • The English summary uplift and the workbook uplift metrics are related, but not identical metric forms.
  • It should be used as a structured reference case, not as a substitute for a site-specific pilot.

Replication pattern for new pilots

  • Define treated and untreated zones before application.
  • Keep panel count and orientation differences explicitly documented.
  • Track per-panel or per-string output, not just site-total energy.
  • Carry the result into ROI logic only after labeling the evidence type.

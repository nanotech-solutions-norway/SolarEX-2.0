How Titan works

Photocatalysis plus superhydrophilic rinsing

The Titan mechanism is built around TiO₂ particles that become active in sunlight. When illuminated, electrons and holes form highly reactive hydroxyl radicals and superoxide species. These oxidize organic contaminants into harmless by-products such as water and CO₂.

After activation, the surface becomes superhydrophilic. Instead of discrete droplets, water forms a thin sheet across the surface, improving rinsing and reducing marks, streaks, and retained organic film.

Real-world case study

Scandinavian utility project: Norwegian solar farm.

The SolarEX case narrative describes a 10 MW Norwegian solar-farm pilot in which a 1 MW treated section was compared with an untreated control group over a six-month winter-heavy period. The commercial signal in this narrative is not just higher energy output; it is the combination of cleaner glass, lower wash intensity, lower water demand, and more stable delivered performance through harsh seasonal conditions.

Trial structure

How the case is framed

  • Utility-scale Nordic photovoltaic context with harsh weather exposure.
  • Treated section compared with untreated control over a winter-heavy operating period.
  • Commercial narrative focused on energy yield, cleanliness retention, and lower maintenance burden.
  • Primary mechanism attributed to the Quartz SiO₂ easy-clean surface.

Operational outcome

What changed in the narrative

The case describes treated panels maintaining higher output through seasonal exposure while adjacent controls degraded more quickly between natural cleaning events. The narrative also states that rain-driven cleaning was materially improved, reducing the need for manual washing and lowering annual water usage by approximately 75%.

Energy value

Performance translated into revenue

For the treated 1 MW section, the cited case framing translates the uplift into approximately 100–200 kWh additional daily energy and roughly €30–€60 additional daily revenue at the European electricity assumptions used in the supporting material.

Maintenance value

Cleaning reduction matters as much as kWh

Where panel cleaning is labor-intensive, weather-sensitive, or water-constrained, the maintenance side of the case becomes strategically important. That is why the case should be read as a combined output-and-O&M story rather than as a yield uplift claim in isolation.

Executive readout

This case is useful because it speaks the language of operators: fewer interventions, lower cleaning burden, stronger output retention, and credible next-step logic for pilot replication. It is not a substitute for a local pilot, but it is strong enough to justify structured technical-commercial evaluation.

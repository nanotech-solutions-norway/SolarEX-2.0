Two revolutionary coating technologies

Silicon dioxide nanocoating creates a hydrophobic surface that repels water, oil, and dust. Self-cleaning lotus effect keeps panels cleaner with minimal maintenance.

Titanium dioxide coating provides photocatalytic self-cleaning that actively breaks down organic contaminants using sunlight energy.

Technology pathways

Mechanism-led selection for real fouling conditions

The uploaded SolarEX deck frames the portfolio as a two-path system: passive hydrophobic easy-clean protection for SiO₂, and active photocatalytic breakdown of organic contamination for TiO₂.

How SiO₂ works

Transparent nanostructure with lotus-effect cleaning

SolarEX SiO₂ forms a transparent 100–150 nm thick film of silica nanoparticles. Water beads and rolls off, taking contaminants with it.

How TiO₂ works

Photocatalytic action and water sheeting

Sunlight excites TiO₂ electrons and creates hydroxyl radicals. Those radicals decompose organic contaminants into harmless water and CO₂ while water sheets for cleaner rinsing.

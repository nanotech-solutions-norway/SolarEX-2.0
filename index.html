Technology portfolio

Two performance pathways

SolarEX Quartz

SiO₂ nanocoating that delivers hydrophobic and oleophobic easy-clean performance through passive self-cleaning behavior.

  • 100–150 nm coating thickness
  • Water, dust, and oil repellency
  • High durability and UV stability

  • ~100 nm transparent layer
  • Photocatalytic self-cleaning
  • UV inhibitor contribution

Technology overview

Two nanocoating systems aligned to different fouling and maintenance profiles

The uploaded source material supports a dual-product architecture: SolarEX Quartz for hydrophobic protection and passive easy-clean behavior, and SolarEX Titan for active photocatalytic cleaning in sunlight.

SolarEX Quartz | SiO₂

Hydrophobic and oleophobic surface engineering

SolarEX Quartz forms an ultra-thin transparent silica nano-film that reduces contaminant adhesion. Water beads and rolls off the surface, carrying dust and pollutants away through a lotus-effect cleaning behavior.

  • Transparent coating with negligible optical impact
  • Reported coverage guideline: 5–15 mL/m²
  • Approximate dry time: 30 seconds, full cure: ~24 hours
  • Chemical resistance in broad pH operating range

SolarEX Titan | TiO₂

Photocatalytic decomposition of organic fouling

SolarEX Titan uses TiO₂ nanoparticles activated by sunlight. The resulting reactive species help decompose organic contaminants, while superhydrophilic behavior promotes streak-free rinsing through thin-sheet water flow.

  • Active self-cleaning under UV exposure
  • Supports breakdown of organic residues and microbes
  • Superhydrophilic water sheeting after activation
  • Additional UV-screening contribution for exposed materials

Application process

Production-minded implementation path

The source material indicates a practical application workflow suitable for field deployment with limited operational disruption.

1. Surface preparation

Clean the panel surface thoroughly to remove residues and enable optimal bonding.

2. Coating application

Apply by spray or wipe in a controlled thin layer with uniform coverage.

3. Safety and setup

De-energize where needed and align ventilation, PPE, and formulation selection with the operating environment.

4. Cure and return to service

Allow initial dry-down and full cure before exposure to rain or aggressive cleaning.
